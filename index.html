Essay: Is the Decentralisation of Web 3.0 Achievable in its Current State?

        Is the Decentralisation of Web 3.0 Achievable in its Current State?

        Abstract
        Web 3.0 was developed as a decentralised web network and guarantees the decentralisation of user data 
        and the clearing of power structures inherent in Web 2.0 (Alabdulwahhab, 2018). This article evaluates 
        the degree of realisable decentralisation within the blockchain-based Web 3.0 platform, as it explores 
        its usability, regulated token operations, congestion, and environmental and compliance-related issues 
        (Reiff, 2024; Earthjustice, 2022). There is promise of a user-friendly internet, but technical and operational 
        problems still need to improve the full adoption of Web 3.0. The article delineates the advancement and the 
        restrictions in the actualisation of decentralisation, thus, concluding that the substantial gains in technology, 
        regulation, and users' adoption of the technology are prerequisites for the Web 3.0 vision to come to fruition, 
        and as a result, technology, law, and user participation must impute.
